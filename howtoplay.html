📖 How to Play PuzzleWorld

Master the art of puzzle solving with these simple steps

🎯

1. Choose Difficulty

Select from Easy (3x3), Medium (4x4), or Hard (6x6) to match your skill level.

✋

2. Drag & Drop

Click and drag pieces from the tray to the board. Position them in the correct spot.

🎯

3. Snap to Place

Pieces will automatically snap into position when close to their correct spot.

🏆

4. Complete & Win

Place all pieces correctly to complete the puzzle and save your record!

📝 Detailed Instructions

🎮 Getting Started

When you first open a puzzle, all pieces are shuffled randomly in the tray at the bottom. The board shows an empty grid with a faint outline of where pieces should go. Your goal is to move all pieces from the tray to their correct positions on the board.

🖱️ Moving Pieces

To move a piece, simply click and hold on it, then drag it to the board. You can drag pieces from the tray directly to the board, or move pieces already on the board to adjust their position. The piece will follow your cursor until you release it.

✨ Snap Feature

When you release a piece near its correct position, it will automatically snap into place. This makes it easy to position pieces accurately. If you release a piece far from its correct spot, it will return to the tray.

⏱️ Timer & Moves

The timer starts automatically when you move your first piece. Every time you release a piece (whether correct or not), your move count increases. Try to complete puzzles in the shortest time with the fewest moves!

⏸️ Pause Feature

Need a break? Click the Pause button to freeze the timer and prevent any piece movement. Click Resume to continue where you left off.

🔄 New Game

Click the "New Game" button at any time to shuffle the pieces and start over. This will reset your timer and move count.

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Start with corners and edges - They're easier to identify and place first.
  • Look for patterns - Use colors and shapes in the image to match pieces.
  • Rotate mentally - Pieces don't rotate, so focus on the correct orientation.
  • Use the grid - The faint grid lines help you align pieces correctly.
  • Take your time - Accuracy is more important than speed when learning.
  • Track your progress - Check statistics to see your improvement over time.
